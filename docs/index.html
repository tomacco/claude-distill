Architecture

    
Three-Tier Knowledge

    
Only the spine loads into context. Everything else is navigated on demand. Knowledge compresses across tiers — it is never deleted.


    

        

            
Tier 1 — The Spine

            
Always in context. Index pointers with relevance hooks. The map, not the territory.

            
max 80 lines

        

        

            
Tier 2 — Active Knowledge

            
Read on demand. One topic per file. Current, actionable, validated on every access.

            
max 60 lines/file

        

        

            
Tier 3 — Archive

            
Compressed history. Promoted back when recalled frequently. The full record at lower resolution.

            
no limit

        

    






    
Capabilities

    
What it does


    

        

            ◈
            
Self-discovering structure

            
No prescribed layout. Reads YOUR workspace at runtime to discover where knowledge lives — whatever you call your files, whatever your craft.

        

        

            ◈
            
Evolving user model

            
Maps your expertise, communication style, thinking patterns, and growth edges. Every session sharpens the picture. Encodes observations, never judgments.

        

        

            ◈
            
Memory pressure scoring

            
Tracks unconsolidated learnings like sleep debt. Recommends consolidation before context degrades — a power nap for cognitive load.

        

        

            ◈
            
Validate-on-recall

            
Every memory access is a verification pass. Stale knowledge self-heals. Frequently recalled archives get promoted. The system maintains itself.

        

        

            ◈
            
Compress, never discard

            
Zero information loss across tiers. Old knowledge gets denser, not deleted. The full history remains navigable at different resolutions.

        

        

            ◈
            
Profession-agnostic

            
Engineer, designer, researcher, PM — the distillation process works on any craft. Principles are universal. Only the routing is personal.

Common questions

    
FAQ


    

        

            Will it mess with my existing CLAUDE.md or memory files?
            
During install, it asks your permission to add one line to CLAUDE.md (the session monitor reference). That's the only touch point. After that, all distill knowledge lives in its own isolated directory (~/.claude/distill/). It never modifies your memory files, project configs, or any other content you maintain. If its knowledge conflicts with yours, it flags it and asks YOU to decide.

        


        

            How do I uninstall completely?
            
Two commands, total cleanup:

            rm ~/.claude/commands/distill.md
rm -rf ~/.claude/distill/
            
That's it. No traces left. No config to revert. No side effects.

        


        

            Does it consume my context window?
            
The distillation itself runs in a spawned sub-agent — a separate, isolated context. Your main session stays untouched. The only cost in your main context is the signal harvest (a brief scan of the conversation), which is much smaller than the full distillation process.

        


        

            How many tokens does it use?
            
The dispatcher (signal harvest) uses ~500-1000 tokens in your main context. The sub-agent runs separately with its own context — typically 3000-8000 tokens depending on how much was learned. This is a one-time cost per distillation, not per message.

        


        

            Does the knowledge it creates auto-load into every session?
            
Only the spine (SPINE.md, max 80 lines) is meant to be referenced. And even that is not auto-injected into your CLAUDE.md — it lives in its own directory. The sub-agent reads it when distilling. For the knowledge to benefit future sessions, you can optionally reference ~/.claude/distill/SPINE.md from your CLAUDE.md, but this is your choice.

        


        

            What if I already have a knowledge management system?
            
Distill won't interfere. It maintains its own parallel structure. If you want to consolidate — merge distill's findings into your existing system — you do that manually. Distill will flag when its knowledge overlaps or conflicts with your files, giving you the information to decide. You're always the authority.

        


        

            Is the learning global across all my sessions?
            
Yes. This is the core idea. Knowledge lives in ~/.claude/distill/ which is shared across every Claude Code session on your machine. A lesson learned during a morning debugging session improves the afternoon architecture review. Different projects, different repos, different contexts — all benefit from the same accumulated knowledge.

        


        

            What if two sessions run /distill at the same time?
            
Handled. Distill uses a lockfile (.lock) to prevent concurrent writes. If another distillation is in progress, it'll tell you to try again in a moment. Stale locks (from crashed sessions) auto-expire after 5 minutes.

        


        

            Does it work for non-programmers?
            
Yes. The distillation process is profession-agnostic. It discovers whatever knowledge structure you have (or helps bootstrap one). Whether you write code, design interfaces, manage teams, or do research — if you have sessions with friction and learning, distill captures it.
