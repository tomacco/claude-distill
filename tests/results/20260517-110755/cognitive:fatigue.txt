This depends on your specific constraints. Here's the key trade-off:

**Federation** (query multiple services/databases at read time):
- Fresh data, no staleness
- No storage duplication
- But: slower queries, couples reporting to source service availability, complex joins across network boundaries, can put load on production databases

**Materialized views** (pre-compute and store denormalized reporting data):
- Fast reads, predictable latency
- Decouples reporting from source systems
- But: data staleness (refresh lag), storage cost, complexity in keeping views updated, schema drift risk

**Choose federation when:**
- Data freshness is critical (real-time dashboards)
- Report volume is low
- Source schemas are unstable and you don't want to maintain sync logic
- Data volume is small enough that cross-service queries are fast

**Choose materialized views when:**
- Reports are queried frequently or by many users
- Acceptable staleness window exists (even 1-5 minutes)
- Queries span many sources and need complex aggregations
- You need to protect source services from read load
- Query latency SLAs are tight

**Common middle ground:** Event-driven materialized views (CDC/event stream populates a read-optimized store). You get decoupling and near-real-time freshness without polling.
