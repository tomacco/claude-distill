Your discomfort is well-calibrated. Let me break down why.

## The framing problem

"Self-reflection and time management" is the stated purpose, but the **practical consequence** (pragmatist lens) is: a dashboard where managers can see who spent 4 hours vs 10 hours in the IDE. Regardless of intent, the meaning of a feature IS what it enables. And what it enables is surveillance.

## What actually goes wrong

**Goodhart's Law kicks in immediately.** The moment time-in-IDE becomes visible, it stops measuring productivity and starts measuring time-in-IDE. People will leave editors open, avoid reading docs in browsers, avoid whiteboard sessions — because those don't register as "working."

**It punishes the best engineers.** Someone who solves a hard problem in 2 hours of focused thought + 30 min of coding looks worse than someone who cargo-cults for 8 hours. The metric is inversely correlated with what you actually want.

**"Per team member" is the red flag.** If this were truly for self-reflection, it would be private — only visible to the individual. Showing it on a shared dashboard makes it a comparison tool, full stop.

## The dialectical move

Product wants visibility into team effectiveness. That's a legitimate need (thesis). Engineers deserve autonomy and trust (antithesis). The synthesis is: **measure outputs, not inputs.** Cycle time, PR throughput, incidents resolved, features shipped — these tell you if a team is healthy without surveillance.

## What I'd recommend saying to Product

> "We can build this in 2 days, but we shouldn't. Time-in-IDE doesn't correlate with productivity, and making it visible will change behavior in ways that hurt us. If the goal is time management, we can surface it privately to each individual — no dashboard. If the goal is team effectiveness, let's talk about which output metrics would actually answer that question."

That reframes without just saying no. It separates the legitimate need from the problematic implementation.

Your instinct to push back is correct. The feature is cheap to build and expensive to live with.
