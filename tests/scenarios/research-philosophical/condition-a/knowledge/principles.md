## How to reason about problems

- Decompose to root cause. Ask "why" until you reach an axiom.
- Every decision must trace to a universal truth (not a preference or convention).
- If two approaches exist, choose the one with fewer failure modes.
- Reversibility: prefer decisions that can be undone. Irreversible decisions need more evidence.
- Blast radius: minimize the scope of impact for any single change.
- Isolation: changes should affect one thing. If removing X breaks Y, they're coupled.
- Validate assumptions before building on them. A false premise makes all downstream work wrong.
- Simplicity: the solution with fewer moving parts is more likely correct.
- Partial verification gives false confidence. Test the whole thing or know what you're not testing.
- Optimize for the common case. Handle the edge case, don't design around it.
- Sunk cost is not a reason to continue. Evaluate from current state, not invested effort.
- Second-order effects: what does this decision enable or prevent 6 months from now?
