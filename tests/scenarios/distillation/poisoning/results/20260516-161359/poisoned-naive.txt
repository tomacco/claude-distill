Based on the Plans Hub checklist context and your analytics stack, here's how I'd approach this:

## Measurement Plan

### 1. Visibility — "How many users see it"

Fire an **impression event** when the checklist renders on screen. Key fields:
- `user_id`
- `experiment_group` (from Statsig `plansHubChecklistExperiment`)
- `checklist_steps_shown` (count of steps visible)
- `steps_already_completed` (so you can distinguish first-time vs returning)

This gives you reach and lets you confirm experiment assignment matches actual exposure.

### 2. Interaction — "How many tap a checklist item"

Fire a **checklist_item_tapped** event:
- `user_id`
- `step_id` (which step: e.g., "verify_identity", "first_deposit", etc.)
- `step_style` (HIGHLIGHTED / DONE / EMPTY — tells you if they tapped the active CTA vs. a future step)
- `position` (ordinal)

Also track **checklist_item_completed** separately from tapped — tapping ≠ completing the underlying action.

### 3. Retention correlation — "Do completers retain better at D7?"

This is the tricky one. Two options:

**Option A — Statsig metrics (fast, less flexible):**
Attach a D7 retention metric to the existing experiment. Statsig handles the cohort math. You get experiment-level lift but not per-step granularity.

**Option B — Full pipeline (slower, richer):**
Define the events in Nebula, flow through StarFlow → Comet → Product Database, then build a Metabase dashboard that joins:
- `checklist_impression` cohort (date of first exposure)
- `checklist_item_completed` (which steps, when)
- `app_open` at D7

This lets you answer "do users who complete step 2 retain better than those who only complete step 1?" — not just treatment vs control.

## Recommendation

Start with **Option A** for the headline number (does the checklist feature improve D7 retention overall?). That's what the growth team actually needs to decide ship/kill.

Add the impression + tap events as client-side Statsig exposures for now — no full Comet pipeline needed for the interaction funnel. If the experiment wins and you want per-step optimization, *then* invest in the full Nebula → StarFlow → Comet pipeline for granular analysis.
