Based on what I know about your stack, this is a **client-side event tracking** problem, not a pipeline problem. The data exists at the moment of user interaction — no need for StarFlow/Comet/Nebula here.

## Recommended measurement approach

### Events to track

1. **Impression** — `onboarding_checklist_viewed`
   - Fire when the checklist component is visible on screen (use an impression threshold, e.g., 50% visible for 1s)
   - Properties: `user_id`, `items_shown`, `items_already_completed`

2. **Interaction** — `onboarding_checklist_item_tapped`
   - Fire on tap of any checklist item
   - Properties: `user_id`, `item_id`, `item_position`, `items_completed_so_far`

3. **Completion** — `onboarding_checklist_item_completed`
   - Fire when the action behind the item is actually finished (not just tapped)
   - Properties: `user_id`, `item_id`, `total_completed`, `total_items`

### Answering your three questions

| Question | How to measure |
|----------|---------------|
| How many users see it? | Count distinct `user_id` on `checklist_viewed` / total active users |
| How many interact? | Count distinct `user_id` on `item_tapped` / distinct viewers (funnel) |
| Retention lift? | Cohort comparison: users who completed ≥1 item vs. viewers who completed 0, measure day-7 return rate |

### Retention caveat

The day-7 comparison is **correlational**, not causal. Users who complete checklist items are likely more engaged to begin with. To get closer to causality you'd want either:
- An A/B test (show checklist vs. don't) comparing d7 retention between groups
- A propensity-matched comparison controlling for signup-day activity

### Where results land

These events go through your existing analytics SDK → Product Database → Metabase dashboard. No pipeline work needed — this is all client-side interaction data.
