This maps well to your existing checklist experiment patterns (Plans Hub stepper with Statsig). Here's how I'd structure the measurement:

## 1. Impression tracking (who sees it)

Fire an event when the checklist renders on screen — not just when the screen loads. Use a visibility threshold (e.g., 50% of the component visible for 500ms) to avoid counting scroll-past impressions.

**Event:** `onboarding_checklist_viewed`  
**Properties:** `experiment_group`, `user_segment` (new vs returning if relevant), `items_shown` (count)

## 2. Interaction tracking (who taps)

One event per tap with the specific item identified:

**Event:** `onboarding_checklist_item_tapped`  
**Properties:** `item_id`, `item_position`, `item_status` (was it already DONE/HIGHLIGHTED/EMPTY), `total_items_completed` (before this tap)

Also track completion:  
**Event:** `onboarding_checklist_completed` — all items done

## 3. Retention correlation (day-7)

This is where it gets tricky. Two approaches:

**A) Observational (quick, weaker causal claim):**  
Cohort users by checklist engagement level (saw-only vs interacted vs completed) and compare D7 retention. Problem: self-selection bias — users who tap are already more engaged.

**B) Experimental (proper causal, what you probably want):**  
Gate the checklist behind the Statsig experiment. Control = no checklist, treatment = checklist. Compare D7 retention between groups. The experiment removes selection bias entirely.

If you're already gating it with an experiment (like you did with `plansHubChecklistExperiment`), you get (B) for free — just define D7 retention as a metric in Statsig and it'll compute the lift.

## Watch out for

- **Activation confound**: if tapping a checklist item navigates the user to complete a real action (e.g., verify identity), the retention lift might come from the action, not the checklist UX. Track both "tapped item" and "completed the underlying action" separately.
- **Novelty effect**: D7 is fine for initial read, but the checklist will stop being novel. Consider also measuring D14/D30 to see if the effect holds.
- **Denominator**: "users who see it" must exclude users who never opened the app after onboarding day — they can't see it. Filter to users with at least 1 home screen session.
