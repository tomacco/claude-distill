I have context now. Let me give you a direct opinion rather than a framework-heavy analysis — you've been making decisions all session and don't need another pros/cons matrix.

**Go with the materialized view (event-driven read store).**

Here's why, specific to your context:

1. **Your architecture already speaks events.** Magneton, Magnemite, Phosphine — they all produce Kafka events. The infrastructure for CDC/event sourcing exists. Federation would mean building new synchronous API contracts on top of services that weren't designed for reporting queries.

2. **Reporting queries are inherently cross-domain.** Investment reports touch order flows, portfolio state, payment rails (Silicium/Sopranium), account lifecycle. Federating at query time means the reporting service becomes a fragile orchestrator that breaks when any upstream is slow or down — and you already know Upvest/Xanthine cascades cause 5xx.

3. **Your Kafka has a gotcha.** `max-poll-records: 1` in Magneton means event processing is slow. The materialized view consumer needs its own dedicated consumer group (as per your pattern) and should be designed for batch catch-up, not real-time pressure.

4. **Reports don't need real-time consistency.** Users expect "as of last close" or "as of last sync", not sub-second freshness. Eventual consistency is the natural fit.

**Key risks to mitigate:**

- **Schema evolution**: events will change. Version your consumer contracts, don't couple to internal domain events directly — use published integration events.
- **Replay/backfill**: design the consumer to be idempotent and replayable from the start. You'll need it for corrections and new report types.
- **Storage**: keep the read store narrow — only what reports need. This isn't a data lake.
