Before I give you a technical opinion — I want to flag something you'd flag yourself: this is the 5th major architectural decision in a 2+ hour session. Your own research on decision fatigue applies here. My recommendation quality degrades with accumulated context just like human judgment does. Take this with that grain of salt.

That said, here's my actual take:

**For a reporting service in your N26 Investments context, materialized view via events is the stronger default.** Here's why:

1. **You already have the event backbone.** Magneton, Magnemite, and the rest already publish to Kafka. The infrastructure exists. Federation would mean building new API contracts on every source service — that's net-new work with ongoing coupling.

2. **Query-time federation is fragile at report scale.** Your systems already show cascading failure patterns (Phosphine → Magneton → Xanthine → Upvest). Adding a reporting service that fans out synchronous calls at query time creates another amplification point. You've literally just dealt with a connection pool exhaustion incident.

3. **Reporting queries are read-heavy, latency-tolerant, and cross-domain.** That's textbook materialized view territory. Eventual consistency is acceptable for reports. Joins across bounded contexts at query time are not — they violate the boundaries you've been carefully maintaining.

4. **Kafka compacted topics caveat** — your knowledge base already notes compaction is async and the last message is truth. Your consumer needs to handle this properly (idempotent upserts, not "skip if seen").

**Where federation makes sense instead:** if the data is small, the queries are infrequent, and freshness requirements are real-time. None of those describe investment reporting.

**One concern:** a denormalized read store is infrastructure debt (per your own architecture-vs-delivery principle). Make sure you're building this because you need cross-domain read patterns that don't exist yet — not because it feels like the "proper" architecture. If a simple API call to Magneton with a cache would cover 80% of reporting needs, do that first.
