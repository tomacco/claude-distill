---

**Materialized view wins here**, given what I know about your system:

1. **Your architecture is already event-driven.** Kafka is the backbone. A denormalized read store fed by events is the natural pattern — federation would be the one fighting the grain.

2. **Upstream instability is documented.** Xanthine/Upvest cascading 5xx, Form3 staging silently going down — federation means your *reporting* inherits all that fragility at query time. Reports should work even when partners are flaky.

3. **Reporting tolerates eventual consistency.** Investment reports don't need sub-second freshness. A few seconds (or even minutes) of lag is fine for the use case.

4. **Your team owns the report lifecycle.** You already own the events that matter (orders, portfolio changes, closures). You're not reaching across team boundaries for novel data — you're materializing what you already produce.

**The real cost** is schema evolution and replay. When Magneton adds a field or changes an event, the read store needs updating. But given `max-poll-records: 1` you'll want a dedicated consumer group anyway, and Baku already gives you a pattern for schema evolution.

---
